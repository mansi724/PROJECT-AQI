# WHO Global Air Quality Guidelines (2021)

The World Health Organization's 2021 Global Air Quality Guidelines set
recommended exposure limits below which health risks are significantly reduced.
They are health-based reference values (not legally binding standards).

## Recommended guideline levels
- **PM2.5:** annual mean 5 µg/m³; 24-hour mean 15 µg/m³.
- **PM10:** annual mean 15 µg/m³; 24-hour mean 45 µg/m³.
- **NO2 (nitrogen dioxide):** annual mean 10 µg/m³; 24-hour mean 25 µg/m³.
- **O3 (ozone):** peak-season mean 60 µg/m³; 8-hour mean 100 µg/m³.
- **SO2 (sulphur dioxide):** 24-hour mean 40 µg/m³.
- **CO (carbon monoxide):** 24-hour mean 4 mg/m³.

## Context for Delhi
Delhi's winter PM2.5 routinely exceeds the WHO 24-hour guideline (15 µg/m³) by an
order of magnitude. The guidelines also define interim targets so that cities
with high pollution can set progressive, achievable milestones on the way to the
final guideline values.
