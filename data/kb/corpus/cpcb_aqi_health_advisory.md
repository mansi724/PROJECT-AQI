# CPCB National Air Quality Index (AQI) — Categories & Health Advisory

The CPCB AQI condenses eight pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, NO2, SO2, CO, O3, NH3, Pb)
into a single 0–500 index across six categories. The reported AQI is the maximum
sub-index; the pollutant driving it is the "dominant pollutant".

## Categories, range, and health impact
- **Good (0–50):** Minimal impact.
- **Satisfactory (51–100):** Minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people.
- **Moderate (101–200):** Breathing discomfort to people with lung disease
  (asthma), and discomfort to heart-disease patients, children and older adults.
- **Poor (201–300):** Breathing discomfort to most on prolonged exposure.
- **Very Poor (301–400):** Respiratory illness on prolonged exposure; more
  pronounced effects in people with lung and heart disease.
- **Severe (401–500):** Affects healthy people and seriously impacts those with
  existing diseases; may cause respiratory effects even with light activity.

## General health guidance
- At Poor and above, sensitive groups (children, elderly, pregnant women, and
  people with asthma, COPD or heart disease) should minimise outdoor exertion.
- At Very Poor / Severe, everyone should avoid outdoor physical activity, keep
  windows closed during peak hours, use N95/FFP2 masks outdoors, and run indoor
  air purifiers where available.
- PM2.5-dominated pollution penetrates deep into the lungs and bloodstream; it is
  the primary driver of health risk in Delhi's winter smog.
