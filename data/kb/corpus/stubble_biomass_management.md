# Stubble / Paddy-Residue Burning Management (NCR & adjoining areas)

Open burning of paddy straw in Punjab, Haryana and western UP during the
October–November harvest is a dominant external contributor to Delhi's winter
PM2.5 when north-westerly winds transport smoke into the NCR.

## Measures directed by CAQM and state governments
- Promote in-situ management with the **Pusa bio-decomposer** and crop-residue
  management (CRM) machinery (Happy Seeder, Super Seeder, balers).
- Ex-situ use of paddy straw as biomass fuel in power plants and industry, and in
  compressed biogas.
- Financial incentives / disincentives to farmers; monitoring of fire counts via
  satellite (VIIRS/MODIS) with district-level accountability.
- Red-entry / bonus schemes and targeted enforcement in high-fire districts.

## Relevance to ward-level forecasting
When upwind fire counts are high and winds are north-westerly, the biomass-burning
signal (fire_upwind) becomes a leading driver of ward AQI — a regional source that
local Delhi action alone cannot fully mitigate, requiring coordination with
neighbouring states.
