# National Clean Air Programme (NCAP)

NCAP is India's national framework (launched 2019 by the Ministry of Environment,
Forest and Climate Change) to tackle air pollution across cities in a
time-bound, target-oriented manner.

## Key elements
- Covers 130+ non-attainment cities that consistently exceeded national ambient
  air-quality standards for PM10 / PM2.5.
- Original target: 20–30% reduction in PM2.5 and PM10 concentrations by 2024
  (against a 2017 baseline); subsequently revised toward a **40% reduction in
  particulate levels by 2025–26** for many cities.
- Each city prepares a **City Action Plan** covering road dust, vehicular
  emissions, industrial emissions, construction dust, biomass and waste burning.
- Emphasis on source-apportionment studies, expanded monitoring networks, and
  public disclosure of air-quality data.
- Funding tied to performance; central assistance to municipal bodies for
  mitigation (mechanised sweeping, greening, dust control).

## Relevance to ward-level action
NCAP explicitly calls for source-specific, locally-tailored interventions — the
kind a ward-level forecasting + attribution system is designed to inform.
